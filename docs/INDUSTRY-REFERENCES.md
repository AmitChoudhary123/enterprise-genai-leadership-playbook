# Industry Reference Map

This playbook is intentionally practical, but it should stay connected to the external standards and patterns shaping enterprise AI.

## Governance and risk

- NIST AI Risk Management Framework and Generative AI Profile: useful for mapping, measuring, managing, and governing AI risks.
- ISO/IEC 42001: useful as a management-system lens for accountability, policies, roles, and continual improvement.
- EU AI Act: useful for understanding risk-based AI obligations and why traceability, human oversight, and documentation matter.

## Application security and safety

- OWASP Top 10 for LLM Applications: useful for threat modeling prompt injection, insecure output handling, data leakage, excessive agency, and related risks.

## Agent and integration architecture

- Model Context Protocol: useful signal that tool/context interoperability is becoming a serious architecture concern.
- OpenAI Agents SDK concepts such as tools, guardrails, sessions, and tracing: useful reference patterns for operationalizing agent behavior.

## Market and adoption signal

- Stanford AI Index: useful annual reference for adoption, investment, model capability, policy, and societal trends.
- MIT and enterprise AI studies: useful caution that adoption and operating model maturity often lag experimentation.

## How to use references

Do not turn standards into bureaucracy. Use them to ask better delivery questions:

- What risk are we managing?
- What evidence proves the control works?
- Who owns the control after launch?
- How does the control show up in the workflow or runtime?
- What gets monitored after release?
